# Business Idea Validation

## Quick Summary

Two-pronged validation process:
1. **Troy framework** — skillset → pain points → research → categorize → test market → commit
2. **Revenue-first lens** — TrustMRR/AppSumo proof → review gaps → growth signals → Reddit pain → competition filter

Run every idea through both. Usually one survives.

## Workflow

### Stage 0 — Define What You Want
- **Pain business** (solves frustration) vs **Enjoyment business** (provides entertainment)
- Prefer pain. Pain drives payment, retention, and has less competition for attention.
- Know your time/life constraints upfront.

### Stage 1 — Self Skillset Evaluation
Create a tier list: **Good** (someone would pay you) / **Acceptable** (can improve) / **Bad**.
Evaluate: Sales, Design, Content, Marketing, Public Speaking, Technical Literacy, Programming, Finance, Operations, Leadership.
Ideal business = overlap between business needs and your Good skills.

### Stage 2 — Identify Opportunities
Two parallel paths:

**Path A — Chase Pain (Troy)**
- List pain points from your life, work, and people close to you.
- Rate each: Pain level (1-5), Frequency (1-5), Niche (1-5, higher = more niche).
- Prefer niche. Easier to charge more, less competition, faster feedback.

**Path B — Revenue-First (SaaS)**
- Browse TrustMRR / AppSumo. Filter by category. Look for products at $20K+ MRR.
- You're not looking for inspiration — you're looking for **proof the market exists**.

### Stage 3 — Research
For each shortlisted opportunity, answer:

| Question | Source |
|---|---|
| Who are competitors? Pricing? Size? | Web research |
| What's the review gap? | 3.4★ with 40+ reviews = gift. Read 2-star reviews — that's your roadmap |
| What's the growth signal? | +30% MoM in boring niche > $500K MRR flat |
| Is the pain real on Reddit? | Search the problem, not the solution. 800 upvotes > 10 surveys |
| Marketing or Sales company? | Low price + individuals = Marketing. High price + B2B = Sales. Pick one lane |
| TAM / SAM / SOM? | Quick napkin math: # in need × price × frequency. Anchor low |

**Timebox research to 10h per opportunity.** Avoid infinite research loops.

### Stage 4 — Categorize & Pick
Score each opportunity:

| Dimension | Options |
|---|---|
| **Timeline to Ship** | <1 month / 1-3 months / 3+ months |
| **Opportunity Size (SOM)** | Low (<$500K) / Mid (<$1M) / Large (<$10M) / Venture |
| **Confidence** | Low (<10%) / Fair (10-50%) / Likely (50-80%) / High (>80%) |

Rules of thumb:
- Shorter timeline → lower confidence needed
- Bigger ≠ better. Niche with less competition often wins
- Venture scale needs high confidence

### Stage 5 — Test Market
- Build **leanest possible solution** (not MVP — think Google Form + manual work)
- Goal: **10 paying customers**
- Charge something. Free = bad feedback. Use: at-cost pricing, godfather offer, free trial with payment info
- Approach customers with humble transparency, not fake professionalism
- **Know when to fold.** If 100 conversations yield zero interest, kill it

### Stage 6 — Commit
Once validated with paying customers → go all in. Stop exploring other ideas.
