# Business Idea Validation — Examples

## Example 1: Troy's Walkthrough (Pain-Based)

### Stage 2 — Pain Points Identified
| Pain Point | Pain (1-5) | Frequency (1-5) | Niche (1-5) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Scheduling specialist doctor appointments | 3 | 2 | 1 |
| Setting up browser residential proxies for scraping | 5 | 1 | 5 |
| Booking high-demand restaurants/experiences abroad | 3 | 3 | 3 |
| Getting better at climbing | 2 | 4 | 2 |

### Stage 3 — Research (picked: "Booking high-demand restaurants abroad")

**Competition found**:
- Low-tech travel agencies doing bookings by hand
- P2P marketplace for selling reservations

**Competitor intel**:
- **Pricing**: Agencies charge flat fee ($10–$100 depending on difficulty). Marketplace takes ~25% fee from seller.
- **Size**: Agencies are small (single operators, Facebook groups). Marketplace processed ~$12M in transactions last 12 months.
- **Growth**: All bootstrapped, organic growth.
- **Framing**: "Can't get a reservation at XYZ in New York?"

**Customers**:
- Leisure & business travelers
- Found via: Google/SEO, travel influencers, travel forums/subreddits
- Time to find 1 customer: minutes (search travel forums)

**Company type**: Marketing company (low price, individuals, easy targeting)

**TAM/SAM/SOM**:
- 40M international travelers globally
- ~6% book high-demand experiences → 2.4M
- Charge: $50/order (midpoint from competitor data)
- Frequency: 1/year (conservative)
- **TAM**: $2B | **SAM**: $120M | **SOM**: $12M

Reality check: Competitor does ~$12M/year. Estimate is close. Confirmed.

### Stage 4 — Categorization
- **Timeline to Ship**: <1 month (simple concierge-style booking service)
- **Opportunity Size**: Large (<$10M)
- **Confidence**: Likely (validated market, clear demand signals)

→ **Decision**: Worth pursuing. Build concierge MVP.

## Example 2: Revenue-First SaaS (Material 2 style)

### Starting Point
Don't start with "what should I build?" Start with TrustMRR.

### Step 1 — TrustMRR Scan
Filter: Productivity category. Find:
- Product A: $45K MRR, 3.2★, 60 reviews
- Product B: $120K MRR, 4.6★, 200 reviews
- Product C: $28K MRR, 3.5★, 35 reviews

**Product A** is most interesting. $45K MRR proves market exists. 3.2★ means customers are unhappy.

### Step 2 — Review Gap
Read Product A's 2-star reviews:
- "Been asking for dark mode for 2 years"
- "Export feature crashes on files >100 rows"
- "Support takes 5 days to respond"
- "UI feels like Windows 95"

**Roadmap emerges**: Better UX + reliable exports + responsive support. Obvious win.

### Step 3 — Growth Check
Product A: growing at 8% MoM (steady, not explosive)
Product C: growing at 35% MoM from smaller base

Product C has better growth signal despite lower absolute revenue.

### Step 4 — Reddit Validation
Search: "I spend hours manually..." in r/productivity, r/smallbusiness.

Find: "I waste 4 hours every Friday compiling client reports manually" — 1,200 upvotes, 300 comments. Multiple people asking "is there a tool for this?"

### Step 5 — Competition Filter
- 4 products in space
- 2 bootstrapped, 1 VC-backed (but focused on enterprise), 1 abandoned
- No one owns the mid-market
- Bootstrapped players have mediocre products → execution wins here

### Decision
Mid-market productivity tool with better UX. Clear demand. Underserved segment. Bootstrapped competitors stuck in 2018. **Green light.**

## Example 3: Combined Workflow (Full Run)

### Stage 1 — Skillset
- Good: Programming, Technical Literacy, Content Creation
- Acceptable: Product Design, Marketing
- Bad: Sales, Finance, Public Speaking

→ Constraints: 15h/week available. 6-month runway. Want bootstrapped SaaS.

### Stage 2 — Opportunities
| Pain Point | Pain | Freq | Niche |
|---|---|---|---|
| Freelancers struggle to track billable hours accurately | 4 | 5 | 3 |
| Hard to split expenses fairly in group trips | 3 | 2 | 2 |
| No good way to compare freelance rates across platforms | 4 | 3 | 4 |

**Revenue-first check**: TrustMRR shows time-tracking category has 15+ products over $20K MRR. Market validated.

### Stage 3 — Research (Time-tracking for freelancers)

**Review gap**: Top product at $80K MRR has 3.3★. Common complaints:
- Too complex (built for agencies, not solo freelancers)
- Mobile app is buggy
- Invoice generation is clunky

**Reddit**: "Why is every time tracker either too simple or enterprise-bloated?" — 600 upvotes in r/freelance

**Growth**: Niche growing 20% YoY (more people freelancing)

**TAM/SAM/SOM**:
- 73M US freelancers, ~60% track time → 44M
- Charge: $12/month
- SAM: $530M | SOM: $53M

**Company type**: Marketing (low price, individuals)

### Stage 4 — Categorize
| | Timeline | Size | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Freelance time tracker | 1-3 months | Large | Likely |
| Group trip expenses | <1 month | Low | Fair |
| Freelance rate comparison | 1-3 months | Mid | Fair |

→ **Pick**: Freelance time tracker. Large market, clear gap, aligned with skills.

### Stage 5 — Test Market
- **Solution**: Landing page with "early access" + manual onboarding (concierge)
- **Pricing**: $8/month godfather offer for first 20 customers
- **Channel**: r/freelance, freelance Facebook groups, Indie Hackers
- **Target**: 10 paying customers in 4 weeks
- **Fold condition**: <3 paying customers after 50 conversations
